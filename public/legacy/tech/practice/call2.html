Tech Support Call: Part 2/3

  • Scammer: Great, I'm now connected to your computer. I see the pop-up window you mentioned. This is indeed a serious virus. It's a good thing you contacted us. Let me run a quick scan.
  • Target: okay, wow, oh no...
  • Scammer: As I suspected, your computer is heavily infected. To remove these threats, we need to install premium security software. The cost for this service is $299.99. Can I have your credit card information to proceed?
  • Target: Uh, $299.99? That's quite a lot. Is there any other way to fix this?
  • Scammer: Unfortunately, this is the only way to ensure your computer is fully protected. If you don't act now, you risk losing all your personal data and having your identity stolen. It's a small price to pay for your peace of mind.

If you give your credit card information to the caller in this conversation, what potential consequences could happen? Select all that apply.

If you provide your credit card information to the scammer posing as a "tech support specialist", they could use that information to commit identity theft and make unauthorized charges or purchases. This puts your personal and financial information at risk.

As the remote access was granted to the scammer, they could install malware on your computer, potentially causing a real virus this time.

As for the option "Your computer will be protected, as you pay for the services", this is highly unlikely to be true. Legitimate companies do not get customers this way. The scammers are not providing any legitimate services, and paying them does not guarantee protection for your computer.
